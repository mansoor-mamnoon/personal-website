    About Mansoor Mamnoon

    I'm Mansoor — a CS student at Berkeley in the EECS Honors program, graduating 2027. I care about the problems where correctness, performance, and scale intersect: systems that don't drop packets, agents that can't be hijacked by malicious documents, infrastructure that stays fast under load.

    This summer I'm joining Databricks as a SWE intern on the Traffic Platform team — networking, security, and performance engineering for large-scale distributed systems in Go and Rust. Before that I spent a summer at Amazon building real-time IoT infrastructure. Outside of industry work, I build projects: an MCP security proxy for LLM agents, a low-latency C++20 matching engine, and a multi-cloud serverless IDE.

    Teaching has been a bigger part of my time at Berkeley than people expect. I'm genuinely passionate about CS Mentors, where I've been a Senior Mentor and Content Lead for CS 61C. Writing worksheets on caching and pipelining is not a chore. I find it clarifying.

    I'm actively targeting 2027 new grad roles in distributed systems, infrastructure engineering, networking, storage and query engines, compiler and runtime internals, and secure AI tooling.
